"What is the Knight's Tour?"
"The knights tour is a classic computing puzzle!"
"Can a knight, starting from one spot on the board, land on every square, only one time?"

"It has been proved that for any m × n board with m ≤ n, a closed knight's tour is always possible UNLESS one or more of these three conditions are met:
1 - m and n are both odd 2 - m = 1, 2, or 4 3 - m = 3 and n = 4, 6, or 8.
...The Answer is yes, and in many different ways!""

"Algorithms:"
"1. Brute force - the hard way"
"2. Warnsdorf Rule - Prefer choices that move to the next node with the least connections"
"3. Discrete Math + Quantium Computing! Implement a graph and embed the graph to the a quantium chimera shape and then send it to D-Wave to try and use thier quantum computers"
